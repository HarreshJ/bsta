History of Brooklyn

  • From the 17th century, Brooklyn started as an agricultural land before developing into an important urban center by the 19th century.
  • The Brooklyn Bridge completion in 1883 was a monumental achievement because it enabled more convenient transport from Brooklyn to Manhattan.
  • Prospect Park (est.1867) and designed by the same architect as the one who designed Central Park, quickly became a shared common gathering spot and a nice area to hang around with friends. It includes a large lake (with turtles) and many events like a concert area and ice-skating rink.
  • The Brooklyn Dodgers (Brooklyn's baseball team) were a beloved part of the city until they relocated to LA in '57.
  • 2012 was a huge sporting turnaround for Brooklyn. The Brooklyn Nets (often condensed to the Nets) moved from New Jersey to Brooklyn, which invoked a new sense of achievement and pride in the locals who found a renewed ambition for sport.
  • Nowadays, Brooklyn is a popular location for its diversity, variety of food and "hipster" culture.
